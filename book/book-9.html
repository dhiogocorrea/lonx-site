Chapter IX

The Tower Opens

Age of the Sword · Year 163

Izanami came into the light for the first time since the making of the world, and the light did not warm her. Nothing had, in a thousand years. She stood in the ruin of the last battlefield, between two exhausted armies, and looked upon Sekai - the world she had helped to make, the world that had gone on and grown lovely without her - with the terrible calm of someone who has finished waiting.

Her first act was to discard Sune. He had opened the door; he was of no further use, and Izanami has never in all her long grief kept a tool past its purpose. Whatever the sword had promised him, whatever greatness he had thrown the blade into the portal to claim, he received none of it. The chronicles do not dwell on his end and neither will this Book. It is enough to say that the man who wanted more than the world would give him received, at the last, exactly nothing - and that of all the punishments in this history, it may be the only one that perfectly fit the crime.

The taking of the Princess

Her second act was the one that began everything the living now suffer to undo. Of all the gathered host - the six peoples, the Alliance, the legion - Izanami reached past every soldier and every general and took the one figure who had held the world together for a hundred and fifty years. She took Kushinadahime. The Princess of Sekai, the keystone of the arch, the Elf who had made three peoples into one and then six - Izanami closed her hand around her and drew her back through the open door, into Yomi, into the dark.

The Alliance surged to stop it and could not. You cannot storm the gate of the dead by force of arms; the living learned that in a single heartbeat. And then, before the door closed, Izanami showed the assembled world what she had built during her thousand years of sealed and sleepless patience. Not an army. Not a weapon. A tower - rising up out of Yomi, floor upon floor upon floor, a whole cold country stacked into the sky of the underworld, and somewhere at the top of it, the Princess.

She had spent a thousand years learning that grief cannot be answered by force. So she built the one thing force cannot solve: a climb.

The challenge

She did not gloat. Grief this old is past gloating. She simply spoke, once, to the whole of the living world at once - a promise and a taunt and, though none of them heard it that way, a confession of everything she was:

For those who fear me, in Yomi I will stay.
If you want your Princess, enter this Portal you may.
There I built a tower where hope is devoured by the hour.
If you are brave, come to Yomi - but death will be no surprise.

Then the door of the world closed, and the tower stood open behind it, and the war that had ended in an instant became a climb that has not ended yet. She had told them, she said, one floor for every decade of her patience. She had lied. She always meant to. There is a floor she did not name, a test outside of time, hidden at the very top - but that is the ending of another book, the one the living are still writing with every step they take upward, and this Book will not spoil what the climbers have not yet earned.

The morning after

The chronicles of the gods end here, at the closing of the door. What comes after belongs to no chronicle, because it has not yet been decided. It belongs to whoever is willing to climb.

And so this Book ends not with a god, but with a soldier - with old Onamuji Firedredger, the Dwarf who argued his island into the first war and buried half his people in the second, who has now watched the Princess he served for a lifetime taken through a door he cannot follow. He does not weep. There is no time to weep; there is a tower, and it is open, and the only way to Kushinadahime leads up through every cold floor of Izanami's grief.

At sunrise, he assembles the first expedition - the Humans and Elves and Dwarves, the Orcs and Half Orcs, even the freed Phantoms who no longer serve the sword - and he looks at the ones who have chosen to climb. He looks, in the end, at you. And he asks the only question that matters at the foot of a thing like this, the question every soul must answer for itself before it takes the first step into the dark:

“Who are you?”

❦ ❧ ❦

Here ends The Book of Yomi.
What follows is not written. It is climbed.
