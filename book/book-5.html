Chapter V

The First Great War

Age of Peoples · Years 105 - 123

The Orc Legion came out of the caves and the border-dark in the hundred-and-fifth year of the world, and Ashihara - young, brilliant, unguarded Ashihara, which had spent its short history building and had never once had to defend - fell in a single season.

The Humans had made a civilization out of hunger and haste, and their haste had never left time for walls that could hold against a thing made purely to break them. Mugdul led the assault, and the great capital of the sun burned under a sky that Amaterasu, bound by her father's promise, could only warm and not defend. The Humans who survived fled north and west in their thousands, carrying what they could and their children first, and for the first time since the world was made, a whole people learned what Izanami had learned alone in the dark: what it is to lose the place you thought was safe.

Three who would not run

It is easy, from the far side of a hundred years, to speak of the Triangle Alliance as though it were inevitable - as though three peoples who had barely spoken were always going to find one another in the ruins. They were not. What happened was that three particular people refused, separately, to accept that the war was already lost, and then had the sense to find each other.

The first was Hiruko Sugitto, a Human, who gathered the scattered refugees of Ashihara and would not let them become merely a fleeing crowd. He turned flight into a march with a direction, and a direction into an army with a purpose, by the sheer refusal to let his people forget they had once built the greatest city in the world and could build it again if they only survived to.

The second was Kushinada Rýnien, an Elf, and of the three she is the one this Book will follow longest, for her story is not finished even now - not while she waits at the top of a tower for the living to reach her. The Elves had felt the border-dark stirring for decades and had been called alarmist for it. When the Orcs came, Kushinada did not say we told you so. She took up the war of a people not her own as though it were her own, because she had understood, before anyone, that this was not a Human war or an Elf war. It was the war Izanami had promised at the mouth of Yomi, arriving at last, and it belonged to everyone alive.

The third was Onamuji Firedredger, a Dwarf, and getting him there at all was its own small miracle, for the Dwarves of Unshu had spent their whole history deciding that the mainland's troubles were the mainland's own. Onamuji broke with that. He argued his stubborn island into the war one hall at a time, and when Unshu finally sailed, it sailed with the full weight of Dwarven craft behind it - engines and ships and shields and the deep patient strength of a people who build everything twice as strong as it needs to be. He is old now, by the reckoning of this Book's final chapters, and he still commands. Remember his name. He will ask you a question before the end.

Three peoples who had believed the world was mostly about themselves discovered, in the worst possible way, that it never had been. The discovery is called the Triangle Alliance. It cost a hundred years and a burned capital to make.

Lake Maystomos

The war turned at the water. Mugdul, first and cruelest of the Orc generals, brought his host to Lake Maystomos expecting to break the Alliance the way he had broken Ashihara - by weight, by terror, by the simple fact that his creatures did not fear death because death was the only inheritance they had ever been given.

Kushinada met him there. The chronicles of the battle are long and this is not the place for all of them, but the shape of it is this: where Mugdul brought fury, the Elf-general brought memory and formation, the slow immortal patience of a people who forget nothing and therefore never repeat a mistake twice in one day. She did not out-rage him. Nothing could. She out-lasted him, and then, when his fury had spent itself against a line that would not break, she ended him. Mugdul fell at Lake Maystomos, and the first and worst of Izanami's weapons was broken.

The march back to Ashihara

Sharog remained, and Sharog was the cleverer of the two. He drew back to the ruin of Ashihara and made a fortress of the very city he had burned, daring the Alliance to spend itself retaking a graveyard. Hiruko took the dare. He led the long march back to the capital of the sun - Humans who remembered building it, Elves who had come to save a people not their own, Dwarves who had left their island for the first time in the history of the world - and before the broken walls of Ashihara, the three peoples together did what none of them could have done alone. They broke Sharog's army. Sharog fell where he had made his stand, and the war that had begun in a single burning season ended, at last, in the eighteenth year of its grief.

The surviving Orcs did not surrender, for they had never been made to understand surrender. They vanished - back into the caves, the border-dark, the thin cold places, carrying their hatred and their hurt into the margins of the world to wait for something that would tell them what to do with it. They would wait a long time. When at last something came, it would not be Izanami. It would be a sword.

The crowning

But that is another chapter. This one ends in light, and the Book insists on it, because there is little enough light in the chapters to come. When the war was won, the peoples of Sekai looked for someone to make its meaning permanent - someone to be the living proof that three peoples could be one, that the Alliance was not merely a wartime accident to be forgotten in peace.

They chose the Elf who had taken up a war that was not hers. Kushinada Rýnien was crowned Kushinadahime, the Princess of Sekai - not queen of any one people, but princess of all of them together, the keystone of the arch that held the three lights up against the dark. It was the first time in the history of the world that Humans, Elves, and Dwarves had agreed on anything at all. That they agreed on her tells you everything about who she was.

❦ ❧

Far below, Izanami watched her weapon broken and her enemies crowned, and she did not rage, and she did not weep. She had all the time in the world; she was the reason time could end at all. She simply began again - slower this time, and cleverer, and aimed not at a city but at the one thing she had learned the living could never resist.

Not fear. Not grief. Ambition. The same door Thrallian had opened. She only had to find the next heart that held one, and this time she would not make a weapon of flesh, which could be broken at a lake. This time she would make a weapon of steel, which could not.
