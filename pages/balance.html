Design spreadsheet

Class balance & skill level requirements

The tuning reference for all eleven classes. Every combat number is expressed as an index where 1.00 is the server baseline (a mastery-capped Warrior at the level cap). The second half of the page lists the character level at which each skill unlocks and the feat needed to push it to Level Master, following the Skill Leveling rule (Level I–X + Master, trained by feats).

Reading the sheet

How the indices work

Index scale

  • DPS — sustained single-target output vs. the Warrior baseline (1.00).
  • EHP — effective hit points (HP × mitigation); Guardian anchors the ceiling at 1.60.
  • Sustain — self/party healing and cleansing throughput.
  • Utility — buffs, control, exploration, and party-key value.
  • Mobility — reposition, gap-close, and kite potential.

Balance targets

  • No class may exceed 1.25 in any single combat axis without paying for it elsewhere.
  • Every class must reach ≥ 0.35 in at least two axes — no pure one-trick builds.
  • Burst carriers (Assassin, Mage) trade EHP for their damage ceiling.
  • A full party of five should sum to roughly even coverage across all five axes.

Tuning notes

Open balance levers

  • Assassin and Mage sit at the 1.25 / 1.20 damage ceiling; if burst feels oppressive, cut their EHP before touching DPS.
  • Ultimate feats gate at Lv 78–84 on purpose — they should land near the top floors, not before.
  • The Weapon Mastery Rule (30% → 100%) means an unmastered class plays at ~60% of these indices; pace early floors around that.
  • Re-check every index after any change to race multipliers on the Races page.
