Core systems

These are the systems that make LONX feel different from a standard grind-based MMORPG. Each one ties progression back to the world and to cooperation between players.

Progression

How players grow

Mastery

Skill Leveling

Skills have Level I through Level X and Level Master. Points alone are not enough: the player must train the skill through explicit feats. Example: Bash may require successful uses, critical hits, and defeating a specific stronger monster with Bash.

Ultimate

Demi-God

A rare, once-per-day burst state. It increases strength, speed, and recovery for a limited duration, letting a character punch above their level in a decisive moment - then it goes on a long cooldown.

Recovery

Rest

A special skill learned from Mitsuhime Kozu on Floor I. It begins slowly to prevent abuse in combat, then scales into a powerful downtime recovery skill.

Expedition

Campaign systems

Gating

Party Keys

Each field and dungeon drops a piece of the floor's Final Dungeon Key. Every party member must complete the key to enter the Floor Boss dungeon - no free-riding into boss fights.

Persistence

Colonies

After a boss is defeated, a Sekai colony follows the players and establishes services on the next floor when possible. Some floors have no true city, creating pressure and scarcity.

Server campaign

Floor Conquest

Floors are conquered once per server and stay conquered. The tower visibly changes as the frontier advances - safe roads open, vendors arrive, and the story moves forward for everyone.

Why it works

Systems reinforce the pillars

Feats over farming

Skill feats mean the strongest players are the most practiced, not just the ones who ground the longest. Mastery is a story of what you have done.

You need the party

Party keys and colony services push players to travel, craft, and fight together. The systems make the interdependence pillar unavoidable.
