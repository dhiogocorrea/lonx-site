Canon decisions

How this edition resolves the draft

The original 2014 notes had multiple alternate timelines, mixed Portuguese/English sections, incomplete floors, and placeholder names. This edition keeps the spirit and the names while making the story coherent and publishable.

Ruling set

The binding decisions

When the draft contradicted itself, these are the choices this bible treats as canon.

  • Timeline normalized: the main canon uses the Roman-numeral timeline: Elves in year 12, Dwarves in year 16, Humans in year 24, and the Tower event in year 163.
  • Onamuji stays alive: because the opening needs him as the expedition commander, the "funeral" clue is reassigned to an earlier memorial ritual rather than Onamuji's death.
  • The tower has twelve floors: Izanami claims one floor per decade, but the twelfth is a secret floor outside normal time - The Final Test.
  • Graphic draft elements are softened: the dark origin of the Orcs remains tragic and corrupted, but is rewritten in publishable dark-fantasy language without explicit detail.
  • Kurixuxa becomes Scylla: the first-floor city takes the stronger name already hinted at in the later notes.
Reasoning

Why each ruling was made

One spine for time

The draft carried at least two dating schemes. A single Roman-numeral timeline gives writers a fixed spine so quests, NPC ages, and flashbacks never contradict one another.

A living commander

The opening cinematic ends with Onamuji asking the player "Who are you?" That beat only works if he is alive at year 163, so his death was moved to an in-world memorial for the fallen of the First War.

The lie of the tower

Izanami says one floor per decade. Making the twelfth floor secret turns her own boast into a plot twist: she lied, and the true test waits outside time. It gives the campaign a real climax.

Publishable tone

The Orc origin (an Elf, Thrallian, corrupted through captivity and cruelty) stays tragic and central, but is described in implication rather than explicit horror so the bible can be shown to partners and platforms.

Stronger names

Placeholder names from the draft are replaced with the more evocative options the later notes already suggested - Kurixuxa becomes Scylla, the first safe base.

One language

Mixed Portuguese and English sections are unified into English for the bible, while original names and terms are preserved intact.
