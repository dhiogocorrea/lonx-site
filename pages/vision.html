Game identity

What LONX Online is

An MMORPG about climbing a mythic tower where every floor is both a physical zone and a memory wound. The player is not the lone chosen one: the whole server is an expedition trying to turn a broken alliance into a living society inside Yomi.

Elevator pitch

The one-line promise

Climb the tower the death-goddess built. Rescue the Princess. Decide whether the cursed sword LONX is destroyed, sealed, or redeemed - and whether the ghosts it made ever get to be people again.

Everything below expands that single promise into the pillars, loops, and audience the game is built for.

Foundations

Core fantasy & design pillars

Every race, class, job, and floor exists because of Sekai's history. The design keeps world-building and mechanics inseparable.

Core fantasy

Players enter Yomi after Izanami kidnaps Kushinadahime, the Princess of Sekai. The objective is to reclaim each floor, build safe colonies, defeat floor bosses, collect keys, and reach the hidden final test where the fate of LONX is decided.

Design pillars

  • World first: every race, class, and job exists because of Sekai's history.
  • Progress by mastery: skills level through feats, not only points.
  • Guild interdependence: jobs create real economy loops.
  • Server campaign: the tower changes as players conquer it.
Detail

The four pillars in depth

Pillar I

World first

Race and class change dialogue, reputation, and which story scenes you witness. An Orc walking into Tsurui Reflection hears different voices than an Elf. Lore is not flavor text bolted on afterward - it is the reason mechanics exist.

Pillar II

Progress by mastery

Skills climb from Level I to Level Master, but points are not enough. Each skill demands feats: a number of successful uses, criticals, or a specific tougher kill. Mastery is earned in the field, not bought at a trainer.

Pillar III

Guild interdependence

Miners feed forgers, herbalists feed apothecaries, farmers feed cooks, catalysts empower enchanters, and refiners unlock the high tier. No single player can supply an entire raid alone - a healthy guild is a supply chain.

Pillar IV

Server campaign

Floors are conquered once per server and stay conquered. Colonies follow the expedition upward, vendors appear, patrols open safe roads. The tower is a shared story with a real ending, not an infinite treadmill.

Audience & tone

Who it is for

Player fantasy

For players who want cooperation over competition: crafters, tanks, healers, and explorers who feel essential. Solo play is possible, but the tower is designed to reward a coordinated expedition.

Tone

Mythic dark fantasy drawing on Shinto myth (Izanagi, Izanami, the III Gods). Themes of grief, obsession, and redemption. Dark, but written in publishable language - tragedy is implied, never gratuitous.

Shape

A party-based persistent-world MMORPG. Instanced boss dungeons gated by party keys sit inside open, shared floors where colonies grow and the economy lives.

Non-goals

What LONX deliberately is not

  • Not a solo grind: the interdependence pillar would collapse if one player could self-supply everything.
  • Not an infinite treadmill: the campaign has a real ending; live service continues through world events, not level inflation.
  • Not gratuitous: the Orc origin and Yomi's horrors stay tragic and implied, never explicit.
  • Not lore-optional: mechanics without world reasons are cut.
