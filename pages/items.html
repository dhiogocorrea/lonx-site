Gear & artifacts

Items that matter

Items should be more than stats. The strongest gear is tied to bosses, jobs, blueprint rarity, and the moral history of the world.

Legendary & story

Artifacts of the tower

Items that carry the narrative as much as the numbers.

Story artifact

LONX

The central cursed blade. It stores power, creates Phantoms, and serves as Izanami's voice into Sekai. Its fate is the ending of the game: sealed, destroyed, or redeemed.

Legendary weapon

Dragon Sword

A legendary forgeable two-handed sword. Requires the Dragon Sword Blueprint and high-tier Forging mastery.

Rare blueprint

Dragon Sword Blueprint

A rare blueprint dropped by the Abyssal Dragon. It disappears after use and carries a meaningful failure chance - a real gamble for a Forger.

Faction relics

Sun, Sea & Moon Relics

Faction relics used to unlock class quests, floor events, and the Triangle Alliance trials.

Progression items

Keys & survival gear

Gating

Final Dungeon Keys

Composite keys assembled from floor dungeons and fields. They gate the boss dungeon and require party-wide participation.

Survival

Teleport Stone

A survival artifact given at the start. It allows return to Scylla or home with a ten-second cast and a cooldown.

Design note

Rarity with weight

The best items carry consequence. Blueprints disappear on use and can fail; legendary crafts demand rare drops and mastery. Rarity is not just a color on a tooltip - it is a decision a guild makes together about where to spend its luck.
