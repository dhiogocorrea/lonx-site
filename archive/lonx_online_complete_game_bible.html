Game identity

What LONX Online is

An MMORPG about climbing a mythic tower where every floor is both a physical zone and a memory wound. The player is not the chosen one alone: the whole server is an expedition trying to turn a broken alliance into a living society inside Yomi.

Core fantasy

Players enter Yomi after Izanami kidnaps Kushinadahime, the Princess of Sekai. The objective is to reclaim each floor, build safe colonies, defeat floor bosses, collect keys, and reach the hidden final test where the fate of LONX is decided.

Design pillars

  • World first: every race, class, and job exists because of Sekai's history.
  • Progress by mastery: skills level through feats, not only points.
  • Guild interdependence: jobs create real economy loops.
  • Server campaign: the tower changes as players conquer it.
Canon decisions

How this edition resolves the draft

The original notes had multiple alternate timelines, mixed Portuguese/English sections, incomplete floors, and placeholder names. This edition keeps the spirit and names, while making the story coherent.

  • Timeline normalized: the main canon uses the Roman-numeral timeline: Elves in year 12, Dwarves in year 16, Humans in year 24, and the Tower event in year 163.
  • Onamuji stays alive: because the game opening needs him as the expedition commander, the funeral clue is assigned to an earlier memorial ritual rather than Onamuji's death.
  • The tower has twelve floors: Izanami claims one floor per decade, but the twelfth is a secret floor outside normal time: The Final Test.
  • Graphic draft elements are softened: the dark origin of the Orcs remains tragic and corrupted, but is rewritten in publishable dark-fantasy language without explicit detail.
  • Kurixuxa becomes Scylla: the first-floor city receives the stronger name already hinted in the later notes.
Narrative bible

The finished story

The campaign is told through opening cinematics, character creation lore, floor-clearing videos, NPC arcs, and server-wide colony progression.

Prologue I - Creation of Sekai

In the beginning, Sekai belonged only to Izanagi and Izanami. They shaped the world, loved it, and almost destroyed it through grief. When Izanami was bound to Yomi, Izanagi returned to Sekai carrying the impurity of the underworld. He washed himself in a divine lake, and from that water were born the III Gods: Susanoo, Amaterasu, and Tsukuyomi.

The III Gods created the first peoples of Sekai and promised to interfere as little as possible. But Izanami watched from Yomi. Every festival, every alliance, every birth looked like betrayal. She could not leave Yomi, so she learned to poison dreams.

Prologue II - The I Great War

Izanami's first plan creates the Orcs and turns them into a weapon against Sekai. Ashihara falls, Humans flee, Elves are shaken by the evil leaking from Yomi's Gate, and Dwarves are forced to leave their isolation. Hiruko Sugitto, Kushinada Rýnien, and Onamuji Firedredger form the Triangle Alliance.

Kushinada defeats Mugdul at Lake Maystomos. Hiruko leads the march back to Ashihara, where Humans, Elves, and Dwarves defeat Sharog's army. The war ends, the remaining Orcs vanish into caves, and Kushinada is crowned Kushinadahime, Princess of Sekai.

Prologue III - The LONX

Years later, Izanami discovers that concentrated hatred near Yomi's Gate can open a path back into Sekai. She guides Trusius to a dragon tooth and then to Sune, an ambitious forger who dreams of becoming more than a craftsman. Sune forges the dragon tooth into LONX, a blade beautiful enough to be worshipped and dangerous enough to hear.

The sword gives Sune power and a voice: Izanami's. Every soul claimed by LONX strengthens the blade. Those transformed by it become Phantoms, spectral warriors bound to Sune's command. The Phantom Legion marches, and the II Great War begins.

Prologue IV - The Tower Opens

At Yomi's Gate, the reunited Triangle Alliance stands with Humans, Elves, Dwarves, Orcs, Half Orcs, and even Phantoms who no longer understand what they are fighting for. Sune reaches the portal and throws LONX into it. Izanami returns to Sekai, then discards Sune and takes Kushinadahime into the tower she built during her imprisonment.

For those who fear me, in Yomi I will stay.
If you want your Princess, enter this Portal you may.
There I built a tower where hope is devoured by the hour.
If you are brave, come to Yomi - but death will be no surprise.

At sunrise, Onamuji asks the player one question: Who are you?

Chronology

Canon timeline

The old draft had contradictory dates; this consolidated timeline keeps the main beats and gives the story a clean spine.

0

Sekai is born

Izanagi and Izanami awaken as the first divine beings and shape the world that will later become the battlefield of gods, races, and shadows.

1

Izanami is bound to Yomi

A divine tragedy sends Izanami to the underworld. Izanagi enters Yomi to retrieve her, but returns alone, carrying the stain of that realm.

3

The III Gods

Izanagi purifies himself in a sacred lake. From the divine water and the shadow of Yomi arise Susanoo of the Sea, Amaterasu of the Sun, and Tsukuyomi of the Moon.

12

The Elves

Tsukuyomi creates the Elves, giving them long memory, speed, moonlit senses, and a bond with nature.

16

The Dwarves

Susanoo creates the Dwarves, strong, stubborn, practical, and able to shape stone, steel, ships, and song.

24

The Humans

Amaterasu creates the Humans, adaptable and ambitious, who build Ashihara and turn steel into civilization.

54-57

The birth of the Orcs

Izanami corrupts the heart of an elf named Thrallian. From captivity, cruelty, and hatred, the first Orcs are born: Mugdul and Sharog.

105-123

The I Great War

The Orc Legion takes Ashihara. Hiruko, Kushinada, and Onamuji form the Triangle Alliance. Mugdul falls at Lake Maystomos, Sharog falls at Ashihara, and Kushinada becomes Kushinadahime, Princess of Sekai.

151

The Dragon Tooth

Izanami discovers how to send concentrated darkness back through Yomi's Gate. She guides Trusius to a dragon tooth and Trusius brings it to the ambitious forger Sune.

154

The LONX is forged

Sune forges the sword LONX. Izanami speaks through the blade, and the first Phantoms are created as Sune's spectral legion.

156-158

The II Great War begins

Sune attacks Ashihara and Unshu, then clashes with the Orcs at Tsurui Forest after betraying Zeakgul. Lugdum leads the Orcs into the Triangle Alliance.

161

The march to Yomi

Kushinadahime gathers Humans, Elves, Dwarves, Orcs, Half Orcs, and Phantoms near Yomi's Gate.

163

The Tower opens

Sune throws LONX into the portal. Izanami returns, discards Sune, takes Kushinadahime into Yomi, and challenges Sekai to climb her tower.

Rules

Core systems

These are the systems that make LONX feel different from a standard grind-based MMORPG.

Skill Leveling

Skills have Level I through Level X and Level Master. Points alone are not enough: the player must train the skill through explicit feats. Example: Bash may require successful uses, critical hits, and defeating a specific stronger monster with Bash.

Party Keys

Each field and dungeon drops a piece of the floor's Final Dungeon Key. Every party member must complete the key to enter the Floor Boss dungeon.

Colonies

After a boss is defeated, a Sekai colony follows the players and establishes services on the next floor when possible. Some floors have no true city, creating pressure and scarcity.

Rest

A special skill learned from Mitsuhime Kozu on Floor I. It begins slowly to prevent abuse in combat, then scales into a powerful downtime recovery skill.

Demi God

A rare once-per-day burst state. It increases strength, speed, and recovery for a short duration and should be treated as a clutch tool rather than a normal rotation skill.

Teleport Stone

Given by Onamuji at the beginning. It returns the player to Scylla or home after a cast time; being hit cancels the cast but does not consume the stone.

Pets

Pets are character-bound companions earned through quests, events, or purchase. Some can fight, heal, or serve as mounts. The Nimbus Cloud is the first canonical example.

Gear & artifacts

Items that matter

Items should be more than stats. The strongest gear is tied to bosses, jobs, blueprint rarity, and the moral history of the world.

LONX

A story artifact and the central cursed blade. It stores power, creates Phantoms, and serves as Izanami's voice into Sekai.

Dragon Sword

A legendary forgeable two-handed sword. Requires the Dragon Sword Blueprint and high-tier Forging mastery.

Dragon Sword Blueprint

A rare blueprint dropped by the Abyssal Dragon. It disappears after use and carries a meaningful failure chance.

Teleport Stone

A survival artifact given at the start. It allows return to Scylla or home with a ten-second cast and a cooldown.

Final Dungeon Keys

Composite keys assembled from floor dungeons and fields. They gate the boss dungeon and require party-wide participation.

Sun, Sea, and Moon Relics

Faction relics used to unlock class quests, floor events, and the Triangle Alliance trials.
